# Accessibility Checklist for Conference at SGH Warsaw School of Economics

## Physical & Mobility Access
- **Step-free access to all key areas**  
  - Entrances, registration desks, lecture halls, breakout rooms, dining areas, and restrooms.
- **Wheelchair‑accessible routes clearly marked**  
  - Avoid steep ramps, narrow corridors, or heavy manual doors.
- **Elevators in working order**  
  - Check capacity, signage, and whether they require staff assistance.
- **Reserved seating for wheelchair users and companions**  
  - Ensure good sightlines and easy entry/exit.
- **Accessible restrooms**  
  - Wide doors, grab bars, turning radius, and proximity to main event spaces.
- **Accessible parking or drop‑off zones**  
  - Close to the main entrance with clear signage.

---

## Hearing Accessibility
- **Microphones for all speakers and audience Q&A**  
  - Enforce their use; many speakers forget.
- **Assistive listening systems**  
  - Induction loops, FM systems, or Bluetooth‑based solutions.
- **Captioning options**  
  - Live CART captioning for plenaries or at least automated captions for streamed sessions.
- **Quiet spaces**  
  - Helpful for attendees with sensory sensitivities.

---

## Visual Accessibility
- **High‑contrast signage**  
  - Large fonts, clear icons, and consistent placement.
- **Accessible presentation materials**  
  - High contrast slides, readable fonts, no text‑heavy visuals.
- **Digital materials compatible with screen readers**  
  - PDFs tagged properly; alt text for images.
- **Wayfinding support**  
  - Tactile floor indicators if available; otherwise, clear maps and volunteers.

---

## Cognitive & Neurodiversity Considerations
- **Clear, simple schedules**  
  - Avoid jargon; provide both visual and text‑only versions.
- **Predictable navigation**  
  - Consistent signage and room naming.
- **Quiet rooms**  
  - A low‑stimulus space for breaks.
- **Session pacing**  
  - Build in buffer time to avoid overwhelming transitions.

---

## Communication & Information Access
- **Multiple formats for all key information**  
  - Printed, digital, and verbal announcements.
- **Accessible website and registration system**  
  - WCAG‑compliant forms, clear instructions, and contact for accessibility requests.
- **Pre‑event accessibility questionnaire**  
  - Ask attendees what accommodations they need.

---

## Food & Break Areas
- **Accessible buffet layout**  
  - Lower tables, space for wheelchair turning, staff assistance if needed.
- **Clear allergen labeling**  
  - Simple icons and consistent placement.
- **Seating variety**  
  - Chairs with backs, space for mobility devices, quiet corners.

---

## Safety & Emergency Planning
- **Accessible emergency exits**  
  - Step‑free routes and evacuation chairs where needed.
- **Staff trained in assisting disabled attendees**  
  - Especially for evacuation, guiding visually impaired guests, and communication support.
- **Clear emergency announcements**  
  - Both audible and visual alerts.

---

## Staffing & Volunteer Preparedness
- **Accessibility training for volunteers**  
  - How to guide someone, how to communicate respectfully, what not to do.
- **Dedicated accessibility desk**  
  - A visible point where attendees can request help.
- **On‑site troubleshooting team**  
  - Someone responsible for fixing barriers quickly (e.g., blocked ramps, broken microphones).

---

## Final Touches That Make a Big Difference
- **Test the full attendee journey**  
  - Walk the route as if you were a wheelchair user, a blind attendee, or someone with sensory sensitivities.
- **Provide a detailed accessibility guide**  
  - Maps, photos of entrances, elevator locations, restroom details.
- **Ensure hybrid/remote options**  
  - Useful for people who cannot travel or need flexibility.
